Dua is the brain of prayer

It is the law of nature that the needy turns to Allah for his need and the distressed to get rid of his troubles. Fitrat-e-Salimah (the understanding of right and wrong) demands that a person should present all his needs and requirements in the court of Allah. However, while presenting your request to him, it is necessary to keep in mind the respect of that Darbar-e-Ilahi.

فَٱذْكُرُونِىٓ أَذْكُرْكُمْ وَٱشْكُرُوا۟ لِى وَلَا تَكْفُرُونِ
remember Me; I will remember you. And thank Me, and never be ungrateful.
Surah Al-Baqarah - 2:152

Importance of Dua

Prayer is the soul of worship and its brain, prayer is worship. In Surah Baqarah, Allah Ta'ala has taught his servants to pray and has told the way to pray, Irshad-e-Khudavandi says, "O Prophet! When my servants seek me from you, say (toh) I am near." When someone calls me, then (toh) I listen to the call of the caller" means Allah Ta'ala accepts the supplication. It is narrated from Hazrat Abdullah bin Umar that Huzoor said, for the one for whom the door of prayer was opened, all the doors of mercy were opened for him and the most beloved of all to Allah is that the servant prays for his health. It is said in a Hadith that prayer is the weapon of a believer, just as a weapon is a means of protection from the enemy, similarly a weapon is a means of protection from the enemy, similarly prayer is also a means of protection from trouble.
The way the importance, virtue and liking of Dua is known in the Quran and Hadith, in the same Hadith, Allah Ta'ala's displeasure has also come for not praying.
But the displeasure of Allah Ta'ala has also come.
It is mentioned in the Hadith that the person who does not pray to Allah, Allah gets angry with him because praying to Allah is a sign of arrogance.

Status of acceptance of prayer(Timings)

It is strictly necessary to accept prayers at all times, but there is more expectation of acceptance in different situations and circumstances.

  • Shab-e-Qadr i.e. in the last Ashra of Ramadan(Last Ten days);
  • Throughout the days and nights of Ramadaan;
  • the night of Eid-ul-Fitr;
  • on the day of Arafah;
  • In Muzdalifah;
  • Night and Day of Friday (It is mentioned in the Hadith that there is an hour on the day of Friday in which the prayer offered is accepted);
  • Between Azan and Iqamat;
  • After the fard prayer;
  • After Tilawat Quran;
  • After drinking Abe Jam Jam;
  • At the time of Ijtema of Muslims;
  • at the time of rain;
  • At the time of Tawaf;
  • Inside Baitullah;
  • In the days of Arafat, Muzdalifah and Mina

Those people whose prayers are accepted.

  • Prayer of the oppressed;
  • Prayer in times of trouble;
  • parent's prayer for the children (whether he is serving or disobedient);
  • Traveller's prayer;
  • Dua of the fasting person at the time of Iftar;
  • Dua of Haji and Motamir (performers of Umrah);

Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported:
The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Three supplications are answered without doubt. The supplication of the oppressed, the supplication of the traveller, and the supplication of the parent for his son."

There are 3 ways how allah will answered your Dua(Supplication)

  • Got what he asked for;
  • He didn't get what he asked for, rather Allah saved him from accident or form big trouble;
  • Didn't even get what he asked for, nor did he was saved from any trouble, instead Allah saved his prayers to give him a greater reward in the judgemental day

FAQ
  • Is Islam a peaceful religion?

    Yes, Islam promotes peace and harmony among its followers and advocates for peaceful coexistence with people of other faiths.

  • What is the significance of the Hajj pilgrimage

    The Hajj pilgrimage is a symbol of unity among Muslims and a journey of spiritual renewal and purification.

  • Are all Muslims required to fast during Ramadan?

    Fasting during Ramadan is obligatory for all adult Muslims, with exceptions for those who are ill, pregnant, or traveling.

  • What is the role of women in Islam?

    Women in Islam have equal spiritual status, and their rights and responsibilities are outlined in Islamic law (Sharia).

  • How has Islam influenced art and culture?

    Islam has made significant contributions to art, architecture, literature, and science, leaving an enduring legacy in many fields.
