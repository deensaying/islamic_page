Islam

Islam, which means "submission to God's will," is one of the world's major monotheistic religions. It is rooted in the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad, who received revelations from Allah, known as God in English, over a period of 23 years. These revelations were later compiled into the Quran, the holy book of Islam.

Five Pillars of Islam

Shahada

The Shahada is the declaration of faith and the most fundamental pillar of Islam. It consists of bearing witness and sincerely proclaiming that "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."

Namaaz

Muslims are required to perform five daily prayers facing the Kaaba in Mecca.The act of prayer is a way for Muslims to maintain a direct connection with Allah and seek guidance and forgiveness.

Zakat

Zakat is the obligatory giving of a portion of one's wealth (usually 2.5% of savings and assets) to those in need, particularly the poor and disadvantaged.

Roza

Muslims are required to fast during the month of Ramadan, which is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar.Fasting during Ramadan is a means of self-purification, increased spirituality, and empathy for those who are less fortunate.

Hajj

Hajj is the pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca in Saudi Arabia, which every Muslim who is physically and financially able must undertake at least once in their lifetime. Pilgrims perform a series of rituals and acts of worship, following in the footsteps of the Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) and his family.
